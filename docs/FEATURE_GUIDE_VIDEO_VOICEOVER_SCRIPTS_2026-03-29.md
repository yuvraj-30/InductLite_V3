## 1. Full Product Tour Voiceover

> This walkthrough introduces InductLite from the outside in.  
> We start with the public journey, where a person arrives at a site, signs in, and completes any required induction steps.  
> We then move to the Operations side, where the admin team sees live site activity, manages people, deliveries, resources, and urgent command workflows.  
> Next, we move through Safety and Compliance, where hazards, incidents, actions, inspections, permits, approvals, and communications are handled in one connected operating model.  
> After that, we look at Contractors and Content, which manage readiness, content governance, competency, trust, and comparative insight.  
> We then cover Integrations and Advanced features such as webhooks, channels, Procore, mobile runtime, evidence, and policy simulation.  
> Finally, we finish with Administration, where the company manages users, settings, audit traceability, and scheduled plan changes.  
> The key idea is simple: InductLite starts with site entry, but it carries that event all the way through live operations, compliance, integrations, and governance.

## 2. Phase 1: Public Journey Voiceover

> This phase explains the product from the perspective of first contact.  
> The homepage introduces InductLite as more than a sign-in form. It presents the product as a site access and induction platform with operational follow-through.  
> The login page is the protected gateway for company staff. It supports both direct login and SSO, and it should feel like part of the same product story.  
> The public sign-in flow is the frontline experience used by contractors, visitors, workers, and delivery drivers. This is where identity, induction, acknowledgement, and access records begin.  
> If someone wants to understand the product quickly, this is the best place to start.

## 3. Phase 2: Operations Voiceover

> This phase explains how the site team runs day-to-day operations.  
> The dashboard is the operator home screen, showing what needs attention first.  
> Sites manages the places where access happens.  
> Pre-Registrations prepares people before they arrive.  
> Deliveries and Resources handle day-to-day site logistics.  
> The Live Register is the live people-on-site view, and Command Mode supports urgent response.  
> History, Analytics, and Exports turn operational activity into something the team can search, review, and share.  
> Together, these features form the live operating layer of the product.

## 4. Phase 3: Safety & Compliance Voiceover

> This phase shows how InductLite manages risk and controlled follow-up.  
> Hazards and Incidents capture safety events.  
> Actions track the work required to resolve those events.  
> Inspections and Safety Forms standardize evidence collection.  
> Escalations, Permits, and Approvals support the decisions that require stronger control.  
> Communications Hub helps the team send urgent messages from the same operational system.  
> This is the part of the product that turns site activity into a formal, auditable safety process.

## 5. Phase 4: Contractors & Content Voiceover

> This phase explains the readiness and governance layer behind site access.  
> Contractors manages the organisations working with the company.  
> Templates controls the governed content people see in inductions and workflows.  
> Risk Passport and Competency help the company understand whether people and organisations are ready to work.  
> Trust Graph and Benchmarks provide broader confidence and performance views.  
> These features help the business decide not just who is present, but who is ready and trustworthy enough to be there.

## 6. Phase 5: Integrations & Advanced Voiceover

> This phase shows how InductLite behaves as part of a larger ecosystem.  
> Webhooks and channel integrations send events out to other systems and communication tools.  
> Procore and Prequalification Exchange move contractor and project-related data between platforms.  
> Mobile Ops and Native Runtime support device-assisted workflows.  
> Access Ops explains the trace behind access decisions.  
> Evidence Packs strengthen trust in critical records, and Policy Simulator lets the team test rule changes before rollout.  
> This phase is especially useful for enterprise or technically minded audiences.

## 7. Phase 6: Administration Voiceover

> This phase covers platform governance.  
> Users manages internal access.  
> Audit Log records what changed and when.  
> Settings controls how the product behaves for the company.  
> Plan Configurator manages scheduled plan or packaging changes in a controlled way.  
> These features are less about frontline work and more about company control, traceability, and long-term governance.

## 8. Recording Notes

When recording:

1. speak a little slower than normal conversation
2. pause briefly between feature groups
3. keep the tone practical rather than overly promotional
4. use the shorter phase scripts if you want a concise walkthrough
5. use the full product tour script if you want one continuous narrated overview
