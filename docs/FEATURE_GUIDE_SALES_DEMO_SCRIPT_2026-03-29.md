## 1. Thirty-Second Version

> InductLite is a site access and induction platform for construction and maintenance teams. It starts with a guided public sign-in flow, then gives the company a live operational view of who is on site, what risks need attention, and what records they can export or audit later.

## 2. Two-Minute Sales Version

> Most site teams still split this work across paper sign-in sheets, separate inductions, chat messages, and spreadsheets.  
> InductLite brings that together into one operating system.  
> A person arrives, signs in through a guided public flow, completes any induction steps, and immediately becomes visible to the operations team.  
> From there, the admin side handles live registers, hazards, incidents, approvals, permits, exports, integrations, and the audit trail.  
> The result is a product that is easier to run day to day and easier to defend when an incident, audit, or customer question shows up later.

## 3. Best Demo Order For A Prospect

1. homepage
2. public sign-in flow
3. live register
4. incidents or approvals
5. exports or audit log
6. one integration page if the buyer is enterprise-focused

## 4. What To Emphasize In A Sales Demo

### Simplicity

> The frontline workflow is simple for the person arriving at site.

### Operational visibility

> The admin team sees activity immediately instead of waiting for someone to update a spreadsheet or radio in.

### Compliance traceability

> Safety, approvals, and history are tied to the same operating record.

### Enterprise readiness

> Exports, audit logging, integrations, and policy controls are already part of the product story.

## 5. Suggested Five-Minute Sales Talk Track

> I’ll show you this in a very practical order.  
> First, here is the public entry experience. This is what a contractor, visitor, or worker sees when they arrive on site.  
> Instead of a paper sheet, they move through a guided digital sign-in and induction workflow.  
> The moment they complete that process, the operations team can see them in the live register.  
> That register becomes the real-time view of who is on site and what needs attention.  
> If a risk or incident appears, the safety side of the product picks up that same record through hazards, incidents, actions, permits, or approvals.  
> And if the company later needs evidence, it has exports, audit history, and integrations available from the same platform.  
> So the value is not just digital check-in. The value is one connected system from arrival through operations and compliance.

## 6. Buyer-Friendly Closing

> The easiest way to think about InductLite is that it starts with access control, but it turns that access event into a useful operational and compliance record for the whole company.
