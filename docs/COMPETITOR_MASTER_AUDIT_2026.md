## Detailed Feature Audit

## 1Breadcrumb

Pages reviewed:

- Homepage: `https://www.breadcrumb.co/en-au`
- Feature page: `https://www.breadcrumb.co/en-au/product/inductions`
- Solution page: `https://www.breadcrumb.co/en-au/solutions/for-head-contractors`

### Inductions

![1Breadcrumb Inductions](./images/1breadcrumb-inductions.png)

- Feature Purpose: Moves paper-heavy morning inductions into a mobile-first workflow with competency and approval records.
- UI Insights: Strong split layout, dark green CTA, generous whitespace, and a report-table mockup that immediately says "admin control". It feels operational rather than decorative.
- User Workflow:
  1. Worker receives a link or scans a code before arrival.
  2. Worker completes induction content and submits competency evidence on mobile.
  3. Site admin reviews status in the induction register and clears the person for site.
- InductLite Opportunity: Keep this level of focus, but make the QR-to-complete path even more explicit and visibly faster.

### Head Contractor Control

![1Breadcrumb Head Contractors](./images/1breadcrumb-head-contractors.png)

- Feature Purpose: Gives head contractors one place to standardise site rules, supplier compliance, and workforce visibility.
- UI Insights: Role-targeted page structure is excellent. The copy speaks to a buyer type, not just a feature list, and the page holds attention with proof-first hierarchy rather than design tricks.
- User Workflow:
  1. Head contractor configures project requirements and induction rules.
  2. Subcontractors upload documents and complete required pre-start tasks.
  3. Site leadership monitors live compliance and follows up exceptions.
- InductLite Opportunity: Add tighter NZ-specific operational proof and faster setup messaging to outperform the more generic regional framing.

## Strategic Summary

### Visual Density Spectrum

- Lowest density: Worksite, ThinkSafe
- Low-to-medium density: 1Breadcrumb, SaferMe, Site App Pro
- Medium density: SignOnSite, EVA Check-in, Sine, SwipedOn, Sitemate
- Highest density: HammerTech, SiteConnect, SiteDocs

### Patterns Repeated Across The Market

- Almost everyone sells the category with phone or dashboard mockups on the right and copy on the left.
- The best direct competitors use one dominant CTA above the fold, not many equal actions.
- Role and industry packaging is a strong conversion pattern. HammerTech, 1Breadcrumb, SignOnSite, Site App Pro, and SiteDocs all use it well.
- Adjacent visitor-management players feel smoother in onboarding, but weaker in construction credibility.
- NZ incumbents often have functional value but weak visual systems.

### What Competitors Do Better Than InductLite Today

- 1Breadcrumb is still the cleanest at one-path hero hierarchy.
- HammerTech is strongest at making a complex construction buyer feel operationally safe.
- SignOnSite is strong at turning a use case into a memorable headline.
- Sitemate is good at making workflow configurability visible, not abstract.
- EVA Check-in and SwipedOn are smoother at making entry workflows feel easy for first-time users.

### Where InductLite Can Beat The Entire Set

- Show a real QR-to-clearance path faster than anyone else.
- Combine 1Breadcrumb's discipline with better bento storytelling and clearer NZ deployment proof.
- Present live site state, permit state, and induction state more visibly than the static dashboard mockups most competitors rely on.
- Avoid the builder-era clutter common in Worksite, ThinkSafe, SiteDocs, and parts of SiteConnect.
- Avoid the generic enterprise tone that softens Sine and adjacent visitor-management players.

### Recommended Visual Direction For InductLite

- Hero: one dominant action, one softer secondary action, one proof rail
- Product proof: real UI earlier, less stock-photo dependence
- Tone: industrial clean core, not generic SaaS gloss
- Surface system: restrained flat/elevated product panels, not decorative layer stacks
- Story flow: site entry -> induction -> compliance clearance -> live visibility
