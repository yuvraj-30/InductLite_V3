## 1. One-Sentence Product Summary

> InductLite is a site access and induction platform that takes someone from public sign-in all the way through live site visibility, safety control, compliance follow-up, exports, and audit traceability.

## 2. Two-Minute Version

If you need the shortest explanation, say this:

> InductLite is built for construction and maintenance teams that need to control who comes on site, what checks they complete, and what the admin team can see and act on afterward.  
> A person arrives, signs in through a guided public flow, completes any induction steps, and immediately becomes visible to the operations team.  
> From there, the admin side handles live registers, hazards, incidents, approvals, permits, exports, integrations, and the audit trail.

## 3. How To Present The Product In The Right Order

Always explain it in this order:

1. public journey
2. operations
3. safety and compliance
4. contractors and content
5. integrations and advanced
6. administration

That order works because it follows the real-world story:

1. someone arrives
2. the site team sees them
3. risk and compliance are managed
4. contractor and content quality are controlled
5. the product connects to the wider ecosystem
6. the company governs the platform

## 4. Five-Minute Demo Script

### Opening

> I’ll show you InductLite in the same order a real customer experiences it. We’ll start with the public side, then move into the admin side where the site team actually runs operations.

### Public Journey

> The homepage explains that this is not just a sign-in form. It is an operational platform for site entry, inductions, and live oversight.  
> The login page is where internal staff move into the protected operating side.  
> The public sign-in flow is the frontline experience. That is where a contractor, visitor, or driver actually checks in, completes the required steps, and becomes visible to the site team.

### Operations

> Once people are entering the system, the Operations features take over.  
> The dashboard shows what needs attention.  
> Sites manages the places where access happens.  
> Pre-Registrations prepares people before they arrive.  
> Deliveries and Resources handle day-to-day operational logistics.  
> Live Register is the live people-on-site view.  
> Command Mode is the urgent-response console.  
> History, Analytics, and Exports turn the operational record into something the business can search, review, and share.

### Safety & Compliance

> This is where the product becomes a real risk-control tool.  
> Hazards, Incidents, and Actions track safety issues and follow-up.  
> Inspections and Safety Forms standardize evidence collection.  
> Escalations, Approvals, and Permits make sure the hard decisions are documented and controlled.  
> Communications Hub lets the team push out urgent operational messages from the same system of record.

### Contractors & Content

> This layer manages readiness and trust.  
> Contractors is the master list of external organisations.  
> Templates governs the content people see.  
> Risk Passport, Competency, Trust Graph, and Benchmarks help the company understand whether people and contractors are ready, qualified, and behaving as expected.

### Integrations & Advanced

> These features show that InductLite can work as part of a larger ecosystem.  
> Webhooks and channel integrations let it talk to other systems and communication tools.  
> Procore and Prequalification Exchange move contractor and project data in and out.  
> Mobile and Native Runtime support device-assisted workflows.  
> Access Ops, Evidence Packs, and Policy Simulator help the team understand system behavior, prove record integrity, and test policy choices before rollout.

### Administration

> Finally, the Administration layer gives the company control over the platform itself.  
> Users manages who can get in.  
> Audit Log shows what changed and when.  
> Settings defines company behavior.  
> Plan Configurator manages scheduled packaging or plan changes in a controlled way.

### Closing

> The easiest way to think about InductLite is that it starts with a person arriving on site, but it does not stop there. It carries that event all the way through live operations, safety, compliance, integrations, and governance.
