## 1. Training Goal

By the end of onboarding, a new operator should understand:

1. how a person enters the system
2. where live operational work happens
3. where safety and compliance follow-up happens
4. where settings, users, and history are managed

## 2. Recommended Training Order

Run training in this order:

1. public sign-in story
2. dashboard and live register
3. daily operations pages
4. safety and compliance workflows
5. contractor and readiness pages
6. integrations and advanced views
7. administration and settings

## 3. Trainer Script

### Opening

> We’re going to learn the product in the same order the work happens in real life. A person arrives, completes sign-in, appears in the live admin view, and then the team manages anything that needs action from there.

### Public story

> Start by understanding the public sign-in flow, because that is the source of a lot of the information you’ll use later. This is how people arrive, identify themselves, complete required checks, and create the first operational record.

### Dashboard and live operations

> Once that public event exists, the Dashboard and Live Register are the most important places to work from. The dashboard helps you decide what needs attention. The Live Register tells you who is on site and whether anyone needs follow-up right now.

### Daily operating pages

> Sites, Pre-Registrations, Deliveries, Resources, History, Analytics, and Exports are the pages that support the day-to-day running of the environment. These are the pages you use to set people up, keep records tidy, and produce operational outputs later.

### Safety and compliance pages

> Hazards, Incidents, Actions, Inspections, Escalations, Permits, Safety Forms, Approvals, and Communications all sit on top of the same operating record. That matters because it means you are not jumping between disconnected tools when something needs a real response.

### Contractor and readiness pages

> Contractors, Templates, Risk Passport, Competency, Trust Graph, and Benchmarks help the company understand whether people and organisations are ready, compliant, and trustworthy enough to be active in the environment.

### Administration

> Users, Audit Log, Settings, and Plan Configurator are the governance layer. These are for controlling who can use the platform, how it behaves, and what traceability exists when someone asks what changed.

## 4. What A New Operator Should Practice

1. logging in
2. reviewing the dashboard
3. filtering the live register
4. finding a historical sign-in
5. creating one preregistration
6. logging one incident or hazard
7. reviewing one approval or permit item
8. exporting a report

## 5. Trainer Notes

### Focus on real-life stories

Frame the training around scenarios like:

- a contractor arriving on site
- a delivery arriving unexpectedly
- a visitor needing approval
- an incident that needs follow-up
- a manager needing an export

### Keep the product story connected

Do not teach each page as if it exists alone. Keep repeating the through-line:

> public sign-in -> live operations -> follow-up -> audit and export
